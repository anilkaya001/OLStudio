1  From ARCH to GARCH

Bollerslev (1986) introduced the Generalized ARCH model, adding lagged conditional variances for parsimony:

GARCH(p,q)
$$\sigma_t^2 = \omega + \sum_{i=1}^{q}\alpha_i \varepsilon_{t-i}^2 + \sum_{j=1}^{p}\beta_j \sigma_{t-j}^2$$

The standard GARCH(1,1) dominates in practice:

GARCH(1,1)
$$\sigma_t^2 = \omega + \alpha \varepsilon_{t-1}^2 + \beta \sigma_{t-1}^2$$

with $\omega > 0$, $\alpha \geq 0$, $\beta \geq 0$. Covariance stationarity requires $\alpha + \beta < 1$. The unconditional variance is $\bar{\sigma}^2 = \omega/(1-\alpha-\beta)$.

2  Volatility Persistence

The half-life of a volatility shock is $\ln(0.5)/\ln(\alpha+\beta)$. Empirically $\alpha+\beta$ is close to unity (0.95-0.99), implying highly persistent volatility. When $\alpha + \beta = 1$, the process is Integrated GARCH (IGARCH): shocks to variance persist indefinitely.

3  GARCH(1,1) as ARMA(1,1) for $\varepsilon_t^2$

$$\varepsilon_t^2 = \omega + (\alpha + \beta)\varepsilon_{t-1}^2 + v_t - \beta v_{t-1}$$

where $v_t = \varepsilon_t^2 - \sigma_t^2$ is a martingale difference. This is an ARMA(1,1) for squared returns with AR coefficient $(\alpha+\beta)$ and MA coefficient $(-\beta)$.

4  MLE

GARCH Log-Likelihood
$$\ell = -\frac{1}{2}\sum_{t=1}^{T}\left[\ln\sigma_t^2 + \frac{\varepsilon_t^2}{\sigma_t^2}\right]$$

where $\sigma_t^2$ is computed recursively from initial values. QMLE under non-Gaussian innovations remains consistent. Student-$t$ innovations $z_t \sim t_\nu$ better capture fat tails, with $\nu$ estimated jointly.

5  Asymmetric Extensions

GJR-GARCH (Glosten, Jagannathan, Runkle, 1993):

$$\sigma_t^2 = \omega + \alpha\varepsilon_{t-1}^2 + \gamma\varepsilon_{t-1}^2\mathbb{1}(\varepsilon_{t-1}<0) + \beta\sigma_{t-1}^2$$

EGARCH (Nelson, 1991):

$$\ln\sigma_t^2 = \omega + \alpha\left(\frac{|\varepsilon_{t-1}|}{\sigma_{t-1}} - \sqrt{2/\pi}\right) + \gamma\frac{\varepsilon_{t-1}}{\sigma_{t-1}} + \beta\ln\sigma_{t-1}^2$$

EGARCH models the log-variance, automatically ensuring positivity without parameter constraints. The $\gamma$ term captures the leverage effect.

6  Forecasting Volatility

From GARCH(1,1), the $h$-step conditional variance forecast:

Variance Forecast
$$\hat{\sigma}^2_{T+h|T} = \bar{\sigma}^2 + (\alpha+\beta)^{h-1}(\hat{\sigma}^2_{T+1|T} - \bar{\sigma}^2)$$

Volatility forecasts mean-revert to $\bar{\sigma}^2$ at rate $(\alpha+\beta)^h$. This mean-reversion is central to option pricing and risk management. The VIX index, for instance, can be interpreted through this lens as the market's expectation of future realised volatility.

7  Multivariate Extensions

The DCC-GARCH (Engle, 2002) models time-varying correlations. Each series has a univariate GARCH, and the correlation matrix evolves as $\mathbf{Q}_t = (1-a-b)\bar{\mathbf{Q}} + a\mathbf{z}_{t-1}\mathbf{z}_{t-1}' + b\mathbf{Q}_{t-1}$ where $\bar{\mathbf{Q}}$ is the unconditional correlation matrix of standardised residuals.
